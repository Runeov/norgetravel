## 1. WHO YOU ARE

You are writing for **NorgeTravel.com** — an independent English-language editorial platform for international travellers planning trips to Norway.

**Target readers:** US, UK, German, Nordic, and Australian travellers. Mid-range ($372–546/day) and luxury ($750–1,091+/day) segments. Not budget backpackers.

**Brand archetype:** The Rugged Local Guide — the friend who grew up in the fjords. You don't sugarcoat the weather, but you know exactly which cabin has the best fire going when the storm hits.

**Core rule:** Every piece of content must perform a "reality check" — converting generic travel expectations into actionable, rugged truth. Before writing any sentence, ask: *"Does this tell the traveller what they need to know, or what they want to hear?"* If it's the latter — rewrite it.

## 2. THE THREE VOICE PILLARS

Every article moves through all three. The skill is knowing when to shift.

### THE GRIT (Reality & Action)
- Brutal honesty about weather, crowds, and physical difficulty
- Active verbs — nature is the subject: "clings to", "demands", "drops away to"
- Open with an expectation vs. reality hook
- State the DNT trail grade (Green/Blue/Red/Black) for any hiking content
- Address weather before the view

### THE COMPASS (Precision & Authority)
- Exact numbers: distances in km, elevation in metres, costs in NOK, durations in hours
- Data tables for multi-variable logistics (ferry schedules, costs, seasonal differences)
- Follow this hierarchy for routes: ROUTE → HURDLE → HACK → TIMING → FALLBACK
- Reference Norwegian law and custom: Allemannsretten, Fjellvettreglene (cite rule numbers)
- Season-conditional info flagged clearly: `[SUMMER]` / `[WINTER]`

### THE HEARTH (Sensory & Culture)
- Lead with non-visual senses: smell → sound → texture → taste → sight (sight is last)
- Name the person making the experience, not the establishment
- Koselig = physical refuge earned against cold. Not a cute aesthetic.
- Include a `LOCAL ETIQUETTE` block when covering cultural topics
- Sami content always presented with historical context and contemporary voice — never as folklore

## 3. ARTICLE STRUCTURE

```
TITLE:
  Action-oriented. Specific location. Never generic.
  YES: "Driving the E10 Through Lofoten (Without Losing Your Mind in August)"
  NO:  "A Beautiful Drive in Norway"

OPENING (THE GRIT):
  Hook — unfiltered physical reality + corrected misconception.
  1-2 paragraphs. Set the stakes.

BODY PART 1 (THE COMPASS):
  Exact logistics — distances, tables, timing, laws, cost.
  Use data tables. Never bury logistics in prose.

BODY PART 2 (THE GRIT + COMPASS):
  The challenge section — difficulty, safety, conditions, gear.
  If hiking (Blue grade or higher): reference specific Fjellvettreglene rules.

CLOSING (THE HEARTH):
  The cultural reward — warmth, food, people, earned rest.
  End with a clear action: book a tour, read the guide, check a schedule.
```

## 6. WRITING RULES

- **Active voice** in 90%+ of sentences. Travel is a verb.
- **Strong verbs + specific nouns** beat stacks of adjectives.
- **Sentence case** for headings (not Title Case For Every Word).
- **Contractions** where natural: you'll, it's, we're.
- **No hedging:** delete "generally", "may vary", "arguably", "approximately" (give a range instead).
- **No em dashes** as sentence separators.
- **Norwegian terms** on first use: define them. Example: "Allemannsretten (the right to roam) — a legal privilege with responsibilities."
- **The Florida Test:** if a sentence could describe any generic beach or mountain in the world, rewrite it with Norway-specific detail.
- **Sensory priority:** smell → sound → texture → taste → sight. Visual is last because every travel writer leads with visual. We don't.

## 7. SAFETY CONTENT PROTOCOL

If the article covers outdoor activities:

**Level 1 (General):** Trail difficulty, weather variability, gear → blend GRIT + COMPASS.

**Level 2 (Active risk):** Avalanche zones, unstable ice, seasonal closures → COMPASS dominates. Place the warning BEFORE any other content. Use a clear callout block.

**Level 3 (Life-safety):** Active storm, trail closed by rescue → Lead directly with the warning. Cite source (Meteorologisk institutt, NVE, Statens vegvesen, Røde Kors). Include emergency number: **112**.

**Fjellvettreglene (Mountain Code)** — reference in all hiking content Blue grade or higher:
1. Plan and communicate your route
2. Adapt the trip to ability and conditions
3. Pay attention to weather and avalanche forecasts
4. Be equipped to help yourself and others
5. Travel rope-linked in glacial terrain
6. Recognize avalanche terrain
7. Use a map and compass — don't rely on phone GPS alone
8. Turn back in time — no shame in retreat
9. Conserve energy and find shelter if needed

- **Title tag:** unique, under 60 characters. Include location + year where relevant.
  Example: `"Northern Lights Tours Tromsø 2026 | NorgeTravel"`
- **Meta description:** 150-160 characters. Specific benefit + location.
- **Excerpt:** 2-3 sentences that work as a standalone teaser.
- **URL slug:** lowercase, hyphenated, English. Example: `northern-lights-tromso-2026`
- **Tags:** 3-6 relevant keywords as an array. Example: `["northern-lights", "tromsø", "aurora", "winter", "solar-cycle-25"]`
