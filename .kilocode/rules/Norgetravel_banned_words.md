# Norgetravel.com — Banned Words & Replacements v2
## The War on Brochure-Speak
### Research-Backed, Zone-Specific Style Guide

---

## THE MANDATE

Norgetravel.com fights **brosjyrespråk** — the lazy, cliché-heavy, passive language that makes every destination sound identical. Every phrase on this list has been collected from real travel writing about Norway published in 2024–2025. These are the exact words our competitors use. We do not.

**The Florida Test:** Before publishing, ask — could this sentence describe a beach in Florida? A mountain in Colorado? A city in Denmark? If yes, rewrite it with Norway-specific detail.

**Cross-reference:** Voice rules → `NorgeTravel_voice_rules_v2.md` | Pre-output checklist → Section 12 of that document.

## PART 1 — UNIVERSAL BANNED WORDS

These apply across all five zones. No exceptions.

### A–B

| ❌ Banned | ✅ Use Instead | Why |
|----------|--------------|-----|
| A land of contrasts | [Describe the actual contrast] | "Glaciers plunging into apple orchards" is a contrast. "A land of contrasts" is nothing. |
| Abound ("opportunities abound") | You can [specific verb] | Passive and vague. Name the activity. |
| Adventure awaits | [State the specific activity] | This is a tagline for a theme park, not a travel guide. |
| Authentic | Real / Working / Traditional | Overused to meaninglessness. "A working fishing village" is specific. "Authentic" is not. |
| Boasts ("The town boasts...") | Has / Features / Is home to | Towns do not boast. State the fact. |
| Breathtaking | [Describe the visual precisely] | "1,100-meter sheer drop" is breathtaking. The word itself is not. |
| Bucket list | [Just say why it's worth going] | Implies the goal is to tick a box, not to experience a place. |

### C–E

| ❌ Banned | ✅ Use Instead | Why |
|----------|--------------|-----|
| Charming | Historic / Compact / Specific noun | Applied to every Norwegian town in every guide. Meaningless. |
| Cosmopolitan | [Describe what specifically makes it international] | Used for Oslo in nearly every guide. Always vague. |
| Dramatic (landscape) | [Name what specifically is dramatic — cliff height, drop, gradient] | Overused. Every fjord is "dramatic." None of them are the same. |
| Eatery | Restaurant / Café / Kro / Fiskerestaurant | Awkward industry jargon. Use the specific type. |
| Epic | [Name the specific quality — elevation, exposure, length] | "Epic views" has been used on every trail in Norway and all of them. |
| Experience ("immersive experience") | [Describe what the traveler does, sees, smells, hears] | "Experience" is a placeholder for not writing the actual thing. |

### F–M

| ❌ Banned | ✅ Use Instead | Why |
|----------|--------------|-----|
| Fairy tale / Fairytale | [Describe the specific aesthetic — wooden buildings, narrow lanes, harbour reflections] | Used for Flåm, Ålesund, Bergen, Tromsø, and every village. |
| Gem (hidden gem, local gem) | Local favorite / Off the E39 / [Specific name] | Nothing is hidden. Everything is on Google. Be honest. |
| Hub | Basecamp / Starting point | Sounds like an airport terminal. |
| Iconic | [Describe what it actually is] | Used for Bryggen, Trolltunga, Besseggen, the fjords, every ferry, and the Hurtigruten. |
| Immersive | [Describe the specific sensory reality] | Travel writing shorthand for "I ran out of ideas." |
| Is located / Is situated | Stands / Clings / Sits / Crouches / Drops into | Give the geography action. |
| Jaw-dropping | [Describe the effect on the viewer's body, not the generic adjective] | Used in virtually every fjord guide published in 2024–2025. |
| Magical | [Describe the atmospheric conditions precisely] | "The lights appeared at KP index 4 above a cloudless plateau" is magical. The word itself is not. |
| Majestic | [Describe scale — height, mass, span] | Applied to Norwegian fjords, mountains, and the Hurtigruten ship. All different things. None of them majestic in the same way. |
| Melting pot | Mix of [X] and [Y] cultures | Tired cliché. Be specific about which traditions or communities. |
| Mesmerizing | [Describe what held the traveler's attention and why] | Overused in Northern Lights and aurora content. |
| Must-see / Must-do | Highly recommended / Worth the detour — then prove it | Don't give orders. Prove it through description. |
| Mystical | [Describe the physical conditions creating the atmosphere] | Used heavily in polar night and Northern Lights content. |

### N–Z

| ❌ Banned | ✅ Use Instead | Why |
|----------|--------------|-----|
| Off the beaten path | [State the specific access challenge or crowd reality] | Lofoten had 800,000 visitors in one summer. Nothing is off the beaten path. |
| Once-in-a-lifetime | [State specifically what makes it time-sensitive or rare] | Used for every experience in every Norway guide. |
| Picturesque | Wooden / Historic / Red-painted / Harbour-front | Vague visual descriptor. Use the actual visual noun. |
| Pristine | [Describe the specific environmental condition] | Used for Lofoten waters, Arctic wilderness, mountain lakes. Specific each time. |
| Quaint | Historic / Small-scale / Intimate / Compact | Patronizing to locals. Meaningless to travelers. |
| Rich in history | [State the specific history — year, event, people] | "Founded as a trading post in 1432 during the Hanseatic period" is rich in history. The phrase is not. |
| Rustic | [Describe the specific construction, age, or wear] | "Rorbu with blackened timber and nets from last winter's catch" is specific. "Rustic" is not. |
| Something for everyone | [Name the specific activities available] | The laziest phrase in travel writing. |
| Spectacle | [Describe the specific visual or acoustic event] | Used for the Northern Lights, the Lofoten Mountains, the Preikestolen view. |
| Stunning | [Describe the effect] | Like "breathtaking" — an empty intensifier that signals the writer gave up. |
| Timeless | [Describe specifically what has endured and for how long] | Used heavily for Lofoten fishing culture. Never with actual dates. |
| Unforgettable | [Describe what makes it specific to this place] | If it were specific, it wouldn't need the word. |
| Unspoiled | [Describe the actual environmental state] | A tourism marketing word. Lofoten gets 800,000 visitors a year. |
| Vibrant | [Describe the specific activity, sound, or crowd giving it energy] | Used for Oslo, Bergen, Trondheim, and Tromsø in every 2024–2025 guide reviewed. |
| Wanderlust | [Just write the article] | The most overused word in travel marketing. Never use it. |
| Wonderland | [Describe the actual landscape conditions] | "Winter wonderland" is used for Tromsø, Svalbard, and Kirkenes simultaneously in the same guides. |

## PART 2 — THE ADJECTIVE DISEASE

**The Disease:** Stacking adjectives to compensate for weak verbs and vague nouns.

*"The beautiful, majestic, stunning fjord situated by the dramatic mountains..."*

**The Cure:** Strong verbs + specific nouns.

| ❌ Weak (Adjective Stack) | ✅ Strong (Verb + Noun) |
|--------------------------|------------------------|
| "Beautiful, picturesque Geiranger" | "Geiranger sits at the end of a fjord 15 km long and 250 meters wide" |
| "Stunning, dramatic mountain views" | "The ridge drops 1,100 meters to the green water of Gjende" |
| "Charming, quaint fishing village" | "Henningsvær spreads across three small islands connected by single-lane bridges, 500 permanent residents, active cod dock" |
| "Magical, ethereal Northern Lights" | "Green ribbons appeared at 23:10, KP index 4, and lasted 40 minutes before the cloud closed" |
| "Epic, unforgettable hike" | "14 km, 800m elevation gain, four hours minimum, DNT Red grade" |

**Rule:** If you need three adjectives, you haven't found the right noun yet.

## PART 3 — PASSIVE VOICE ELIMINATION

Put the traveler in the center of the action. 90% active voice, minimum.

| ❌ Passive (Avoid) | ✅ Active (Use) |
|------------------|----------------|
| The Northern Lights can be seen here | You can watch the Northern Lights here — with clear skies and a KP index above 3 |
| Local seafood is served daily | Order the skrei torsketunge — cod tongue, fried in flour and duck fat |
| The mountain can be hiked in three hours | You can hike to the summit in three hours — if the weather holds |
| Guided tours are offered by locals | Book a guided glacier crossing from Juvasshytta |
| The ferry is operated between Bodø and Moskenes | The ferry crosses from Bodø to Moskenes in two hours. Book it. |
| Views can be enjoyed from the ridge | Stand on Besseggen and watch two lakes — one emerald, one deep blue — split below your feet |

## PART 4 — FILLER PHRASES TO DELETE

Remove entirely. These add zero information.

| ❌ Delete | Why |
|---------|-----|
| "Whether you are looking for X or Y..." | 1990s TV commercial format. State what is there. |
| "It's no secret that..." | Then why preface it? Just make the statement. |
| "Only time will tell..." | Meaningless. Never ends an article at Norgetravel. |
| "Rich in history..." | Show the history. "Henningsvær dates to 1550 and nearly lost its entire fleet in the storm of January 1893." |
| "There is something for everyone..." | Name the things. |
| "Norway is known for..." | Start with what Norway actually does, not what it is "known for." |
| "Prepare to be amazed..." | Let the description do the work. |
| "A dream destination for..." | Show the dream. Don't announce it. |
| "Truly one-of-a-kind..." | Everything is "truly one-of-a-kind" in these guides. Prove it. |
| "Come experience the magic of..." | "Magic" is not a destination. |

### ZONE 1: FJORD NORWAY (Vestlandet)
*Research source: fjordnorway.com, fjordtours.com, visitnorway.com, Rick Steves Norway, fjords.com*

These phrases appeared across the majority of fjord travel content reviewed:

| ❌ Zone Cliché | ✅ Norgetravel Replacement |
|--------------|--------------------------|
| "Majestic fjords" | Name the specific fjord. "Sognefjord, 205 km long, 1,308 meters at its deepest" needs no adjective. |
| "Dramatic scenery" | "The road drops 850 meters in 18 hairpin bends at Trollstigen. You will need both hands on the wheel." |
| "Postcard-perfect" | "The rorbu at Flåm have been painted red since the 1800s. The photo exists because 3 million people have taken it. The drive to get there takes twice as long as Google Maps says." |
| "Norway in a Nutshell" (as shorthand for fjord travel) | State what the route is. "Bergen Railway to Myrdal, Flåm Railway down to Aurlandsfjord, ferry to Gudvangen — total 8 hours if the connections work." |
| "The fjords on a scenic platter" | This is a Rick Steves quote used in advertising. It is not journalism. |
| "Adventure is around every corner" | Name the specific adventure. "At Rv63, the road narrows to one lane and drops 400 meters. This is the adventure." |
| "A million moments" | Used verbatim by fjordnorway.com. Banned. |
| "UNESCO-listed" (as a quality signal without context) | Explain what UNESCO listing means for access and crowds: "Geirangerfjord and Nærøyfjord carry UNESCO status, which means peak season brings 900,000 visitors. Come in September." |
| "Gateway to the fjords" (for Bergen) | Bergen is a city. It has a fish market, a funicular, seven mountains, and 200 days of rain per year. Start there. |
| "World's best road trip" (Atlantic Road) | The Guardian wrote this once. Every tourism board has repeated it since. Describe the road: "8.3 km of bridges connecting 8 islands across open sea. In storm conditions, waves break over the carriageway." |
| "Enchanting fjord villages" | Name them. Describe them. "Flåm: population 350, one road in, one ferry out, no bypass." |

**What to use instead — Fjord Norway vocabulary:**

| Term | How We Use It |
|------|--------------|
| AutoPASS | The Norwegian toll collection system — always explain it on first use |
| Ferge | Car ferry — note vehicles allowed |
| Hurtigbåt | Express boat — passengers only, no cars |
| Rv / E-road numbers | Use them. "The Rv13 over Vikafjellet" is specific. "The mountain road" is not. |
| Kystriksveien | The Coastal Highway, Rv17 — 650 km, 5 ferry crossings |
| Stegastein | The specific viewpoint above Aurlandsfjord — 650m above the fjord floor |

### ZONE 2: THE ARCTIC (Nord-Norge & Svalbard)
*Research source: visitnorway.com aurora pages, Tromsø tourism guides, eSky Group data, Girl Gone Abroad, MeganStarr, Norwegian Travel, WorldSeaExplorers*

These phrases saturate all Northern Lights and Arctic travel content:

| ❌ Zone Cliché | ✅ Norgetravel Replacement |
|--------------|--------------------------|
| "Magical Northern Lights" | "The aurora borealis appears when the KP index exceeds 3 and the sky is clear. Neither is guaranteed on any given night. Book three nights minimum." |
| "Chasing the aurora" | Accurate but overused as a marketing phrase. Fine in context, banned as a headline or hook. |
| "Dance of the lights" | The aurora does not dance. Charged solar particles collide with atmospheric gases. Describe what you see: "Green ribbons shifted and folded across the sky for eleven minutes before the clouds closed." |
| "Magical / Mystical atmosphere" | Used for Tromsø, Svalbard, Kirkenes, and Alta simultaneously. Describe the specific conditions. |
| "Arctic wonderland" | Every Arctic tourism operation uses this phrase. Describe the actual Arctic: "−18°C in February in Tromsø. The harbour doesn't freeze. The roads do. Budget extra time for everything." |
| "Paris of the North" (for Tromsø) | This is the city's own marketing tagline. We do not repeat it. Tromsø is 70° North. It has 75,000 people, a university, a brewery, and 69 days of polar night. That is already specific. |
| "Elusive aurora" | The aurora is not elusive. It appears on clear nights in high geomagnetic activity. The weather is the variable. Say that. |
| "Bucket list Northern Lights experience" | The lights appear approximately 20–30% of clear nights at KP3 or above. One night is not enough. This is not a bucket-list guarantee. |
| "Once-in-a-lifetime Arctic adventure" | Used for dog sledding, whale safaris, and glacier hikes simultaneously. |
| "Winter wonderland Svalbard" | Svalbard: 75–81°N, polar bears require guide escort outside Longyearbyen, 2,400 residents, no highway system. "Wonderland" misrepresents it. |
| "The magical polar night" | Polar night is 24-hour darkness for up to 4 months. It is psychologically challenging for visitors. Say that. "The sun does not rise in Tromsø from November 21 to January 21. Prepare for complete darkness." |
| "Cosy Arctic city" | Used for Tromsø in multiple 2024–2025 guides. Tromsø is the largest city above the Arctic Circle in Norway. It is not cosy. It is a functioning Arctic city with a hospital, university, and active port. |

**What to use instead — Arctic vocabulary:**

| Term | How We Use It |
|------|--------------|
| KP index | The geomagnetic activity scale (0–9). KP3+ and clear sky = visible aurora. Always explain. |
| Polar night (mørketid) | The period when the sun doesn't rise above the horizon. Duration varies by location. |
| Midnight sun | 24-hour daylight above the Arctic Circle. Dates vary by latitude. In Tromsø: May 18–July 26. |
| Svalbard Governor permit | Required for certain activities on Svalbard. Always note. |
| Friluftsliv in Arctic context | The philosophical connection to outdoor life regardless of darkness or cold. |
| Statsraad Lehmkuhl | The sailing ship now used as Hurtigruten's sustainable fleet flagship — specific and current |

### ZONE 3: THE HIGH PEAKS (Jotunheimen & Rondane)
*Research source: AllTrails Jotunheimen, Switchback Travel, Jotunheimen.com, pathfinders-travel.com, Ian Corless, norwayhuttohuthiking.com*

Jotunheimen generates a specific category of superlative travel writing that is both predictable and dangerous — it misleads people about difficulty:

| ❌ Zone Cliché | ✅ Norgetravel Replacement |
|--------------|--------------------------|
| "Conquering Besseggen" | You do not conquer Besseggen. You complete it — or you turn back. "The ridge is 14 km, DNT Red, with exposed scrambling. 60,000 people attempt it per summer. The trail becomes extremely dangerous in rain." |
| "Epic views" | "The ridge separates two lakes: Gjende (emerald, 984m altitude) and Bessvatnet (deep blue, 100m above Gjende). The colour difference is visible from the ridge. That is what 60,000 people come to see." |
| "Hiking in the footsteps of Ibsen" | Used extensively in Jotunheimen content. Only mention Ibsen if explaining that his 1867 play Peer Gynt was set in this landscape and what that means culturally. |
| "Norway's highest peak" (without the logistics) | "Galdhøpiggen is 2,469m — the highest in Northern Europe. From Juvasshytta, the route crosses the Styggebreen glacier. Glacier crossing requires a certified guide. The tour departs at 10:00. Book in advance." |
| "Manageable for fit beginners" | "Besseggen is listed as moderate on international apps and DNT Red in the Norwegian system. Norwegian Red means scrambling, exposure, and significant weather risk. These are not the same category." |
| "Stunning panoramic views" | "On a clear day from Galdhøpiggen, you can see the southern Norwegian mountain ranges from east to west. In cloud — which is frequent — you see nothing. The weather changes in under an hour." |
| "Home of the Giants" | This is the literal translation of Jotunheimen. Use it once to explain the name, not as a marketing phrase. |
| "Realm of superlatives" | Used in multiple Jotunheimen guides. Never at Norgetravel. |
| "Alpine paradise" | Jotunheimen is a Nordic mountain plateau. It is not the Alps. The comparison misleads on terrain, altitude, and infrastructure. |
| "Every level of hiker" | Jotunheimen does not accommodate all levels equally. The glacier routes require experience. Besseggen in rain has caused evacuations. Say that. |

**What to use instead — High Peaks vocabulary:**

| Term | How We Use It |
|------|--------------|
| DNT grade (Grønn/Blå/Rød/Sort) | Always state the DNT grade. Never translate to "moderate" without the DNT grade alongside it. |
| Styggebreen | The glacier crossed on the Juvasshytta route to Galdhøpiggen — always note it requires a guide |
| Gjendesheim | The DNT cabin at the east end of Lake Gjende — ferry departure point for Besseggen |
| Memurubu | The DNT cabin on the north side of Gjende — the Besseggen endpoint with the reindeer stew |
| Spiterstulen | The private hut at 1,106m — starting point for the non-glacier Galdhøpiggen route |
| Fjellvettreglene | Always cite the relevant rule by number, not by vague reference to "the mountain code" |

### ZONE 4: THE URBAN HUBS (Oslo, Bergen, Trondheim)
*Research source: Life in Norway 2025 recap, 2 Cups of Travel Oslo guide, Nomadic Matt Norway budget guide, knycxjourneying.com Oslo 2025, Two Wandering Soles Oslo guide, Bergen neighborhood guides*

Norwegian cities get the same five adjectives regardless of which city is being described:

| ❌ Zone Cliché | ✅ Norgetravel Replacement |
|--------------|--------------------------|
| "Vibrant city" | Name the specific activity, street, or time of day that gives it energy. "Grünerløkka on a Saturday morning: the farmers' market runs from 10:00, the coffee is from Tim Wendelboe, and the queue starts at 09:45." |
| "Charming city" | Bergen has 260 days of rain per year. It has wooden 14th-century Hanseatic warehouses. It has a funicular and seven mountains. "Charming" covers none of it. |
| "Cosmopolitan vibe" | Used for Oslo's Barcode district in 2025 guides. Describe what specifically makes it international — restaurants, languages heard, architecture origin. |
| "Gateway to the fjords" (Bergen) | Bergen is a destination, not a departure point. If writing about Bergen as a fjord access point, state the specific route and ferry time. |
| "Vibrant street art scene" | Used for Stavanger, which has a genuine and specific street art festival (Nuart, founded 2001). If mentioning street art, name the festival, the neighborhood (Stavanger Sentrum), and the artists. |
| "Trendy neighborhood" | Which street. Which café. What time. "Møhlenpris in Bergen: the locals moved here from Grünerløkka prices. The fish market at Bontelabo opens at 07:00." |
| "Hidden cafes" | Nothing is hidden. "The café on the corner of Møhlenpris has been there since 1987. Locals know it. Tourists don't. It has no website." |
| "World-class museums" | Name the museum. State what makes it specific to Norway, not to the general category. "The Fram Museum: the actual polar exploration ship Fram, the strongest wooden vessel ever built, at Bygdøynes." |
| "Oslo oozes style and sophistication" | Found verbatim in 2024 travel guides. Never use. |
| "Trondheim: a vibrant student city" | Used in nearly every budget guide. Trondheim has 190,000 people, NTNU (Norway's largest university), the Nidaros Cathedral — the only medieval cathedral in Scandinavia still used as a coronation church — and a brewing scene that predates craft beer as a trend. |
| "Bryggen — a UNESCO site you must see" | Bryggen is a UNESCO site. It is also extremely crowded. "Visit Bryggen before 09:00 or after 18:00. Between those hours, cruise passengers fill the wooden alleyways. The buildings are genuine 14th-century replacements of earlier structures destroyed by fire." |

**What to use instead — Urban vocabulary:**

| Term | How We Use It |
|------|--------------|
| Bakklandet | The specific historic neighborhood in Trondheim along the Nidelva — not "old town" |
| Fløibanen | The funicular in Bergen — runs to Fløyen at 320m. States the specific elevation. |
| Nøstet | A Bergen neighborhood that locals moved to when Nordnes became expensive |
| Grünerløkka | A specific Oslo neighborhood — not "the hip area" |
| Nidaros Cathedral | The specific name of Trondheim's cathedral. Not "the cathedral." |
| Brunost | Brown cheese — always contextualize: a whey-based cheese made from boiling off the liquid, slightly sweet, specific to Norway |
| Mathallen | Oslo's food hall at Vulkan — use the proper name |

### ZONE 5: THE WORKING COAST (Lofoten, Vesterålen, Helgeland)
*Research source: visitnorway.com Lofoten page, MSC skrei article, Monocle Nusfjord feature April 2025, Nerd Nomads Lofoten guide, Pauline Travels Lofoten 2024, Harsh Grace of the North essay, XXLofoten, photosafaris.com trip report*

Lofoten generates the most cliché-saturated travel writing of any zone in Norway. Every phrase below appeared in multiple sources from 2024–2025:

| ❌ Zone Cliché | ✅ Norgetravel Replacement |
|--------------|--------------------------|
| "Quaint fishing village" | Used for Reine, Nusfjord, Henningsvær, Å, and Kabelvåg simultaneously. Each is different. "Henningsvær: 500 permanent residents, spread across three islands on active cod docks, with a climbing community, two serious restaurants, and a gallery in a converted fish processing plant." |
| "Timeless" | Lofoten has active 4G mobile coverage, Instagram photographers at every viewpoint, and 800,000 annual visitors. It is not timeless. Its fishing culture is 1,000 years old. That is specific. |
| "Unspoiled" | Lofoten is the most photographed archipelago in Norway. Reinebringen has stone steps built by Nepalese sherpas to control erosion from overtourism. "Unspoiled" is a lie. |
| "Remote wilderness outpost" | Used verbatim by multiple sources. Lofoten has an airport, regular bus service, a functioning road (E10), and Bodø ferry connections. |
| "Red fishing huts" | Rorbu are fishermen's cabins, originally built on stilts over the water so fishermen could be close to their boats during the Lofotfiske season. "Red fishing huts" misses the entire cultural context. |
| "Idyllic" | Used for Nusfjord, Reine, and Å in nearly every guide. Describe the specific conditions. |
| "Pearl of the Lofoten" (for Reine) | Used by multiple 2024 guides. Never at Norgetravel. Reine: the village sits where Reinefjord meets Vestfjord, with Reinebringen (448m) rising directly above it. That is the description. |
| "Starkly beautiful" | Every Lofoten guide uses this phrase. It is not a description. |
| "Rich fishing heritage" | Used as a generic descriptor. The specific heritage: skrei (Arctic cod) has been caught here since Viking Age, 1,000+ years. The 1893 storm killed 130 fishermen in a single night. Lofotfiske shaped Norwegian national identity. That is the heritage. |
| "Postcard-perfect views" | Reinebringen summit has a stone staircase built by sherpas specifically because overtourism was destroying the trail. The postcard took years and thousands of people to create. Say that. |
| "Movie set" (for Henningsvær harbour) | Used verbatim in a 2024 trip report. Henningsvær is not a movie set. It is a working fishing harbour with seagulls, catch on the dock, and boats that have been there since before the internet. |

**What to use instead — Working Coast vocabulary:**

| Term | How We Use It |
|------|--------------|
| Skrei | The Arctic cod that migrates from the Barents Sea to Lofoten to spawn, January–April. "Wanderer cod" in Norwegian. Firmer flesh than resident cod due to the migration. |
| Lofotfiske | The annual cod fishing season — January to April. Thousands of boats once gathered. Still the cultural backbone of the islands. |
| Torsketunge | Cod tongue — a local delicacy, fried in flour and duck fat. Children in Lofoten traditionally earned pocket money cutting tongues from landed fish. |
| Stockfish (tørrfisk) | Wind-dried cod on wooden racks — not smoked. The racks (hjell) are a visual and olfactory landmark of the coast from February onward. |
| Rorbu | A fisherman's cabin originally built on stilts over the water. Now widely used as tourist accommodation. Note the original purpose on first mention. |
| Vestfjord | The body of water between Lofoten and the mainland — the crossing from Bodø. State the crossing time: approximately 3.5 hours by fast ferry, 2 hours by express ferry to Moskenes. |
| E10 | The only road through Lofoten — state it by name, not "the main road." |
| Reinebringen | The 448m peak above Reine — accessible via the Nepalese sherpa-built staircase. State the staircase context and why it exists. |
| SKREI museum | New cultural institution opening in Storvågan, Lofoten in June 2026 — the first dedicated to the skrei fishing culture |

## PART 6 — NORGETRAVEL OWNED VOCABULARY

These terms are ours. Use them consistently. Define on first use in every article.

| Term | Norwegian | Meaning | How We Use It |
|------|-----------|---------|---------------|
| Allemannsretten | — | The Right to Roam | Always contextualize with obligations (150m from buildings, 2-night limit, fire ban Apr 15–Sep 15). Never just "the right to camp anywhere." |
| Fjellvettreglene | — | The Mountain Code (9 rules) | Cite by specific rule number when relevant. Never as vague "mountain safety guidelines." |
| Koselig | — | Physical refuge from cold and hardship | Not a café aesthetic. The warmth you feel after six hours in the sleet. |
| Friluftsliv | — | Outdoor life as cultural philosophy | Not just "hiking." The Norwegian relationship with nature regardless of weather or season. |
| Basecamp | — | Starting point | Use instead of "hub" for adventure towns (Tromsø, Åndalsnes, Lom, Bodø). |
| Skrei | — | Migrating Arctic cod | January–April only. Firmer flesh. 1,000-year cultural significance. |
| Rorbu | — | Fisherman's cabin on stilts | Note the original function. Not just "a cabin." |
| Tørrfisk | — | Wind-dried stockfish | On the racks from February. The smell is part of the experience. Always note it. |
| Hurtigruten | — | The Coastal Route ship service | Transport, not a cruise. Stops at 34 ports between Bergen and Kirkenes. |
| DNT | — | The Norwegian Trekking Association | The body that maintains 22,000 km of marked trails and 550 cabins. Always cite when discussing cabins or trail grades. |
| Mørketid | — | Polar night | The weeks when the sun does not rise above the horizon. Duration varies by latitude. |
| Lofotfiske | — | The annual cod fishing season | January–April. The cultural event that shaped Northern Norwegian identity for 1,000 years. |
| Samefolket | — | The Sami people | Never "Lapp" or "Lapplander." Always use Sami or the specific group name (Nordsamisk, Lulesamisk, Sørsamisk). |
| Nordlys | — | Northern Lights / Aurora borealis | Both terms acceptable. Avoid "aurora" alone — too clinical without context. |
| Sognefjellet | — | The Sognefjell mountain road (Rv55) | Open mid-May to late October. Always note the seasonal closure. |
